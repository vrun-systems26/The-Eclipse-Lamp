# Iris Face Extension Construction

 This part of the project was unexpected, as I had forgotten about one problem with the mechanical iris that this construction happened to solve. My main issue was that I couldn't lay the iris mechanism flat against the inner wall of the top shell to fit through the custom hole I made, and I couldn't glue anything directly onto the servo gear since it was rotating, so I had to design something that glued onto the face of the iris plate instead (the one sitting above the circular portion and petals). The distance between the servo gear and the iris top plate was about 5.4mm, with the gear sitting taller; if the gear had been higher than the iris plate, the mechanism wouldn't have fit through the hole, since the gear would be protruding and spinning. I designed the extension at a height of 6.5mm, accounting for that 5.4mm gap plus the 0.8mm wall thickness it needed to pass through, with an extra 0.3mm added just in case. This part would then fit through the hole in the lamp shell, where I'd hot glue it in place to keep it secured against the wall. The iris shutter and camera would still be able to see clearly at the proper angle. It's ironic that a part this simple required this much explaining, but it was the best solution I could come up with to secure the iris mechanism to the inner wall.

Note: Print this in the same color as the lamp shell for the best camoflauge, (Refer to Custom Created CAD Models.md for better information on printing and assembly)
