# [Camera - Iris] Frame Construction

 This part of the project was the frame that held the Pi camera behind the mechanical iris, so that when the iris opened, the camera could capture footage of the room and detect people. When I first started, I noticed that the back of the mechanical iris had 5 holes perfectly placed around a disc meant for screws. I decided to make use of these holes by designing a frame with a camera case that could mount directly to the disc. To do this, I took the exact same disc from the Thingiverse file I had sourced and cut around its sides to make it a bit smaller, reducing print time while still preserving the 5 screw holes. I then found another file on Thingiverse that included a camera case mount STL and sourced that as well (I only used the camera-front model from it). After that, I was able to take the mount and merge it with the 5-screw disc I had cut earlier to create the final frame. I positioned the camera lens as close as possible to the iris blades so it would capture the best possible footage. 

This is the link I used to get the Camera top enclosure: 

https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:404654/files

Note: For the case I only used 1 stl file out of the 4 known as (Pi_camera_case_front.stl) and also 
the 5 screw holes are very small in diameter so if needed you can hot glue or super glue.
