Mechanical Iris Construction    Date: 6/26/26, Time: 9:49 PM 

  This part of the project used a mechanical iris mechanism — a closing system that uses a set of gears (powered by an SG90 servo) to rotate multiple overlapping, curved blades, often called petals, creating a continuously adjustable central opening. I used it to cover and shield a Raspberry Pi camera module, adding to the project's "spy" effect. The servo rotates the petals outward to reveal the largest opening, allowing the camera to capture footage, and rotates them inward to close and hide the camera from view. This lets the Eclipse Lamp conceal the camera so no one realizes a camera is constantly observing and recording the room. For this build, I sourced a 5-petal mechanical iris model from Thingiverse, with a 60mm x 60mm outer diameter and a 40mm inner opening. It includes a mounting hole for the SG90 servo, which attaches to a driving gear meshed with a larger gear that rotates the petals to open and close the central aperture.

This is the link I used to get all 5 parts for the construction: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:5170139/files#google_vignette (Credits to Lore09)

Note: Print it the same color as you printed the lampshade in so it blends in and looks less obvious.

If it is needed and the tolerences are too little and the gears dont mesh properly or the outer rings dont fit properly, sand it down because the SG90 servo can't handle too much friction.
