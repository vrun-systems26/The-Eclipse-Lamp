# Top Lamp Construction |  Date: 7/3/26  |

  This part forms the lamp's top stand, housing the mechanical iris/camera and lamp module. I researched wall thickness for optimal
light diffusion, settling on 0.8mm with white filament, then modeled a hollowed 150mm x 150mm x 150mm cube in Onshape, leaving a 3mm tall
bottom base for later. In Tinkercad, I cut a bottom-center hole for a servo-driven gear (needed since the shaft is too small to attach
directly to the shell) for pan rotation, plus six oval slots 10mm wide and 145mm long for the three wire groups: Pi-to-camera, 
Pi-to-lamp via USB cable, and Pi-to-iris servo. I set the iris lower and flipped it upside down so the camera sits closer to the Pi, since 
its ribbon cable must stretch and twist during rotation; originally both were meant for the top shell to avoid ribbon movement, but
then I couldnt attach the lamp or servo properly. I added a lid, though I may skip it so light shines through the shell. The next thing
I had to do was check printing time, and again it was troublesome. Starting at 9 hours and 56 minutes, I had to change the quality from
0.2 to 0.28 mm layer height, I changed infill to 0 percent, Increased speed from 90 to 115 mm/s, and wall speed from 57.5 to 80 mm/s. The
find and most important thing I did to reduce time, was use Spiralized Outer Contour, or as some would call "Vase Mode". Although this 
reduces the strength of the walls drastically, it lets light shine through better and prints significantly faster. I ended with 5 hours 
38 minutes of print time. 

Note: I discussed this earlier in this passage but print the shell in a light color like white or even translucent for the light to shine
